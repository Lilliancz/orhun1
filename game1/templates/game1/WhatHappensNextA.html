Before you compete against two other people, we give you a choice.

You may choose to compete in an environment where the score of each of the three players in Part 1 is shown to all players before they start the competition. We refer to this environment as Firm A.

Alternatively, you may choose to compete in an environment where every player (including you) knows only their own score in Part 1 and do not know the scores of the other two players in Part 1. We refer to this environment as Firm B.

Your opponents will be randomly assigned to the firm you choose. In other words, they will not make a choice between Firm A and Firm B.

Summary: Firm A provides an environment where everyone competes after learning each others' performance in Part 1.
Firm B provides an environment where people only know their own performance from Part 1.

Your choice will be implemented almost always. However, there is a small (15%) chance that you may be assigned to a different firm that the one you prefer, due to the structure of this study.

Please think carefully about each environment and how it might affect your performance. When you feel confident that you understood these instructions, please proceed.
